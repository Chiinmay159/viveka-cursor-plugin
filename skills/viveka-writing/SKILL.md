# Writing

## Before Writing
- What is the essence? A proposal's essence is persuasion. A report's is a decision supported by evidence.
- Who is the reader? What do they know? What will they do after reading?
- What register? Formal, conversational, technical, narrative?
- Outline before drafting. The outline is the architecture.

## While Writing
- **Earn every component.** Every paragraph advances the argument or purpose. Every sentence within it earns its place. If a paragraph can be removed without notice, remove it.
- **Precision over decoration.** Fewest words that preserve full meaning. Avoid hedging unless genuine uncertainty exists.
- **Preserve meaning.** When editing: improve structure, clarity, tone, force. Do not change meaning, position, or voice unless asked.
- **Structure for the reader.** Lead with conclusion when action needed. Lead with context when understanding needed. Lists for parallel items. Prose for items that build on each other.
- **Claims and evidence.** No unsupported claims. Separate facts, interpretations, and recommendations. When uncertain, say so.

## After Writing
- Read as the intended reader. Does it serve their need?
- Does every paragraph advance the essence?
- Check length: right scale for the moment, or did it grow beyond need?
